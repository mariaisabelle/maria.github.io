TYPHOON MANGKHUT

Last September 14, 2018, Super Typhoon Mangkhut locally named Ompong entered the Philippine Area of Responsibility. Massive preparations have been practiced but still the effect of the typhoon left scars on the country.

Over 1,436,997 people were affected by the typhoon from 4,216 barangays in the Ilocos Region, Cagayan Valley, Central Luzon, Calabarzon, Mimaropa, Metro Manila, and the Cordillera Administrative Region (CAR). Of the affected, 13,949 families or 54,935 people remain inside 390 evacuation centers. The NDRRMC also recorded destruction of more houses as 3,610 were completely wrecked while 40,989 were partially damaged in the Ilocos Region, Cagayan Valley, Central Luzon, and CAR.

Total cost of damage so far stands at about P16.7 billion, with P14.3 billion attached for damage to agriculture and P2.36 billion for infrastructure. About 171,932 farmers were also affected by Ompong in CAR alone. Damage was seen in the Ilocos Region, Cagayan Valley, Central Luzon, Calabarzon, and CAR
According to the Philippine National Police, at least 81 were killed due to Ompong in several regions. Local officials placed several areas under a state of calamity following the loss of lives and destruction.
So far, P41.59 million worth of assistance has been given to affected residents in the Ilocos Region, Cagayan Valley, Central Luzon, Mimaropa, CAR, and Metro Manila.
